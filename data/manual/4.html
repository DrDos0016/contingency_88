The purpose of this plan is to secure an election despite not winning the popular vote. This can be accomplished through creative districting of the cities which make up our great state.

HOW ELECTIONS WORK:


A common misconception held by both the citizens of Roosylvania as well as nation in general is that the winner of an election is simply the candidate that receives the most votes.

The reality is that each city in our state is broken up into various electoral districts. In each district, voters choose between the Red party and the Blue party. The votes for a district are tallied and one point is given to the party that wins. Whichever party wins the most districts in a city wins the city. Whichever party wins the most cities, wins the election.
